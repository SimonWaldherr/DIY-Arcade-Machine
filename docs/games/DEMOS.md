# Demo Showcase (`DEMOS`)

## Gameplay

This zero-player entry exists for attract-mode visuals. It rotates through self-running demos such as Snake, Conway's Life, Spark particles, a fire effect, Langton's ants, a flood-fill maze animation, falling Matrix code, a 3D Starfield, a rolling and pulsing 3D wireframe Cube, an Orbit particle sculpture, a radial Warp star tunnel, a classic Bounce screensaver, a zooming vector Tunnel, a Demoscene Plasma effect, a Mystify screensaver effect, a spiral Vortex, colored Comet trails, a raindrop water Ripple, a Fireworks display, a golden-angle Phyllotaxis spiral, a Lissajous oscilloscope curve, a chaotic double Pendulum, and a self-playing Breakout Arcade attract demo. Press `LEFT` or `RIGHT` on the Joystick to cycle through the effects, and when `CONFIG_ENABLE_GAME_DEMOS` is enabled the selector can also launch short CPU-driven previews for the playable games that remain in the registry. There is no win condition and no score; the purpose is simply to showcase the display and the game engine.

## Technical details

A demo hub owns a list of autonomous simulations and swaps between them on input. In this repository, `DemosGame` stores the enabled demo ids in `self.demos` and cycles them inside its update loop through joystick directions. Each simulation keeps its own compact state because it never needs player input, so the code leans on lightweight structures such as `bytearray` grids, queue-like lists, and simple tuples for positions. Cellular automata and procedural visuals fit the LED matrix well because they produce readable motion at low resolution, which is why `DemosGame` includes Conway's Life, Langton's ants, a flood-fill animation, a fire effect, an auto-playing snake variant, a Matrix digital rain effect, a 3D Starfield simulation, a sine-wave RGB plasma simulation (`PLASMA`), a rotating and scaling 3D wireframe cube (`CUBE`), a layered orbit particle sculpture (`ORBIT`), a radial warp-speed star tunnel (`WARP`), a bouncing logo (`BOUNCE`), a zooming vector tunnel (`TUNNEL`), a vector-driven polygon animation (`MYSTIFY`), a spiral line field (`VORTEX`), colored particle trails (`COMETS`), a particle burst effect (`SPARK`), a damped water-surface ripple field rendered as 2x2 blocks (`RIPPLE`), rising rockets that burst into gravity-bound spark showers (`FIRWRK`), a rotating golden-angle phyllotaxis spiral of sunflower seeds (`PHYLLO`), an oscilloscope Lissajous curve with a drifting frequency ratio (`LISSAJO`), a chaotic double-pendulum simulation that integrates the Lagrangian equations of motion with a fading tip trail (`PENDUL`), and a self-playing Breakout attract demo whose paddle AI eases toward the ball to clear coloured brick rows (`ARCADE`). The playable-game preview list mirrors `GameSelect`, and the active subset is still shaped by `CONFIG_LOW_RAM_MODE` when memory pressure matters.
